Coxinha

Description

Coxinha is one of the most popular and beloved snacks in Brazil. It consists of a flavorful dough filled with shredded and seasoned chicken, shaped into a teardrop (resembling a chicken thigh), breaded, and fried until golden and crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 500g of cooked and shredded chicken breast
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions and parsley to taste
  • 1/2 cup of cream cheese (optional, for extra creaminess)

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups of the chicken cooking broth
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • Salt to taste

For Breading:

  • 2 beaten eggs
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Filling: In a pan, heat the olive oil and saut√© the onion and garlic. Add the shredded chicken, season with salt and pepper, and cook for a few minutes. Turn off the heat, mix in the green onions, parsley, and cream cheese (if using). Set aside.
  2. Dough: In another pan, bring the chicken broth, butter, and salt to a boil. As soon as it boils, add the flour all at once. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the dough pulls away from the bottom of the pan, forming a ball.
  3. Transfer the dough to a smooth, lightly oiled surface. Knead the dough while still warm until it is smooth and even.
  4. Shaping: Take small portions of the dough, flatten them in the palm of your hand, place a teaspoon of the filling in the center, and close it, shaping it into a coxinha (teardrop).
  5. Breading and Frying: Dip each coxinha in the beaten eggs and then coat it well with breadcrumbs.
  6. Fry the coxinhas in hot oil until they are golden brown on all sides. Remove with a slotted spoon and place them on paper towels to drain the excess oil.
  7. Serve hot!
